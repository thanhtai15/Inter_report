### [Event 1 - AWS AI/ML and Generative AI Workshop](4.1-Event1/)

**Date & Time:** Saturday, November 15, 2025, from 8:30 AM to 12:00 PM

**Location:** AWS Vietnam Office

**Role:** Attendee

**Main Content:**
- **Session 1 (9:00-10:30 AM):** AWS AI/ML Services Overview with Amazon SageMaker
  - Data preparation and labeling
  - Model training and fine-tuning
  - Model deployment and MLOps
  - Live Demo: SageMaker Studio

- **Session 2 (10:45 AM-12:00 PM):** Generative AI with Amazon Bedrock
  - Comparing Foundation Models (Claude, Llama, Titan)
  - Prompt Engineering techniques (Chain-of-Thought, Few-shot learning)
  - Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture
  - Bedrock Agents and Guardrails

**Knowledge Gained:**
- Understanding AI-Driven Development Lifecycle (AIDLC) and Human-Centric philosophy
- Mastering end-to-end ML development with SageMaker
- Learning to build GenAI applications with RAG pattern
- Prompt engineering techniques and model selection

**Value Obtained:**
- Reduced development time from 2 weeks to 1.5 days with AIDLC
- Understanding MLOps and production deployment best practices
- Knowledge of Responsible AI and content filtering with Guardrails

- Mastering CI/CD best practices on AWS

### [Event 3 - Mastering AWS DevOps: CI/CD, IaC, and Containers](4.3-Event3/)

**Date & Time:** Monday, November 17, 2025, from 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM

**Location:** AWS Vietnam Office

**Role:** Attendee

**Main Content:**
- **DevOps Principles:**
  - DevOps bridges Development and Operations
  - Automation to reduce human errors
  - DORA metrics, MTTR, Deployment Frequency

- **CI/CD Deep Dive:**
  - CI vs CD vs Continuous Deployment distinctions
  - AWS CodePipeline architecture
  - Automated testing and quality gates

- **Infrastructure as Code:**
  - CloudFormation: declarative, AWS-native, free
  - CDK: programmatic approach, reusability
  - Terraform: multi-cloud, large community, HCL language
  - Best practices: version control, modular design, documentation

- **Container Technologies:**
  - Docker fundamentals and containerization benefits
  - ECS: AWS-managed, tight integration
  - EKS: Kubernetes compatibility, complex but powerful
  - App Runner: Simplest option for containerized apps

- **Best Practices:**
  - T-shaped skills: Deep expertise + broad knowledge
  - Documentation culture: Knowledge sharing
  - Practice over theory: Hands-on learning
  - Soft skills: Communication, teamwork, problem-solving

**Knowledge Gained:**
- DevOps is culture, not just tools
- Importance of fundamental knowledge before learning tools
- Systematic approach to problem-solving
- Project management with Jira/Trello

**Value Obtained:**
- Deep understanding of 3 IaC tools and trade-offs of each
- Container orchestration strategies for different use cases
- T-shaped skills mindset for career development
- Soft skills importance in technical roles
